School Achievements

Inter School Debate on Environmental Pollution
Won first prize in an inter-school debate on environmental pollution.

Environmental pollution is one of the most pressing issues of our time, affecting the health and wellbeing of millions of people and animals around the world. However, not everyone agrees on the causes, effects, and solutions of this problem. That is why a group of middle school students from Glacier Middle School in Buckley, Washington decided to participate in a debate competition on this topic, hoping to raise awareness and persuade others to take action. The students prepared for the debate by researching various sources of information, sucn as books, articles, websites, and documentaries. They learned about the different types of pollution, such as air, water, soil, and noise pollution, and how they impact the environment and human health. They also studied the main causes of pollution, such as industrial activities, transportation, agriculture, and waste disposal. They also explored the possible solutions to reduce pollution, such as using renewable energy sources, recycling, conserving water, planting trees, and adopting eco-friendly habits. The students faced a tough opponent in the debate, a team from another middle school that argued that environmental pollution is not a serious issue and that the solutions are too costly and impractical. The students used their knowledge and skills to counter their arguments with facts and evidence. They showed how pollution affects not only the environment but also the economy and society. They demonstrated how the solutions are not only feasible but also beneficial for everyone in the long run. They also appealed to the emotions and and values of the audience by using stories, examples, and statistics. The students won the debate by convincing the judges and the audience that environmental pollution is a real and urgent problem that requires immediate attention from everyone. They received a trophy and a certificate for their achievement. They also recieved praise and recognition from their teachers, parents, and peers. They felt proud and happy for their success. They also felt motivated and inspired to continue learning and spreading awareness about environmental issues. They hoped that their debate would make a difference in the world.

Inter School Soccer Competition
Came second in inter school soccer competition.

Soccer is a popular sport among middle school students, as it provides fun, exercise, and teamwork. A middle school soccer team from Buckley, Washington participated in an inter-school competition that involved 16 teams from different schools. The team trained hard and practiced regularly, hoping to win the trophy and make their school proud. The team played well in the group stage, winning three out of four matches and qualifying for the quarter-finals. They faced a strong opponent in the quarter-finals, but managed to score a late goal and win 1-0. They advanced to the semi-finals, where they met another tough team. The match was tied at 2-2 after 90 minutes, and went to a penalty shootout. The team showed nerves of steel and scored all five penalties, while their goalkeeper saved one. They reached the finals, where they faced the defending champions. The final was a thrilling and intense match, with both teams playing their best. The team scored the first goal in the first half, but conceded an equalizer in the second. The match went to extra time, where neither team could break the deadlock. The match went to another penalty shootout, where the team missed one penalty and lost by 4-5. They got second place in the competition, which was still a remarkable achievement. The team was disappointed but not discouraged by the result. They recieved a medal and a certificate for their performance. They also recieved applause and admiration from their teachers, parents, and friends. They felt proud and satisfied for their effort. They learned a lot from the competition, such as skills, tactics, sportsmanship, and perseverance. They hoped that they would have another chance to play in the future and improve their results.

Inter School Science Project Competition
Won second prize in inter school Science Project competition.

A middle school from Buckley, Washington participated in an inter-school science fair project competition that challenged students to create innovative solutions for real-world problems. The team chose to work on a project that aimed to reduce plastice waste and pollution by converting plastic bottles into eco-bricks. The team collected plastic bottles from their homes, schools, and communities and cut them into small pieces. They then used a machine that they built themselves to melt the plastic pieces and mold them into bricks. They tested the strength, durability, and insulation of the bricks and compared them with conventional bricks. They also calculated the cost, energy, and environmental impact of their process. The team presented their project at the science fair, where they impressed the judges and the audience with their creativity, skills, and knowledge. They explained how their project could help solve the problem of plastic waste and pollution by transforming it into a useful and sustainable material. They also demonstrated how their bricks could be used to build houses, schools, and other structures in low-income areas. The team won second place in the competition, which was a great achievement. They recieved a medal and a certificate for their work. They also recieved praise and recognition from their teachers, parents, and peers.
